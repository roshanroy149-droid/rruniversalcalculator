How do you calculate X percent of Y?

Multiply Y by X, then divide by 100. For example, 20% of 150 is (150 × 20) ÷ 100 = 30.

How do you find what percent A is of B?

Divide A by B, then multiply by 100. For example, 30 is what percent of 120? (30 ÷ 120) × 100 = 25%.

Where does this come up in real life?

Discounts, tax rates, grade calculations, tip splitting, and comparing changes over time all reduce to one of these two percent problems.
