PM-JAY now covers every senior citizen 70+ for an extra ₹5 lakh, income no bar.

The Ayushman Vay Vandana Card has extended free coverage to 6 crore senior citizens regardless of income — stacking on top of the base scheme's ₹5 lakh to give some families ₹10 lakh in annual cover. Here's how the numbers actually work.

By TallyBench Team · Updated July 21, 2026 · 7 min read

Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY, already the world's largest government-funded health insurance scheme, has expanded further with the Ayushman Vay Vandana Card — giving every Indian citizen aged 70 and above a free additional ₹5 lakh in annual health cover, regardless of household income. Combined with the base scheme's ₹5 lakh family cover, that takes total available coverage to ₹10 lakh a year for households where a senior citizen qualifies.

The short version: PM-JAY provides ₹5 lakh cashless hospitalization cover per family per year at 30,000+ hospitals, covering 55 crore+ people from the bottom 40% of India's population (per SECC 2011 data). Since October 2024, the Vay Vandana Card adds a further ₹5 lakh for every citizen 70+, income no bar — reaching 6 crore senior citizens. The 2026-27 Union Budget increased PM-JAY's allocation to ₹9,500 crore, up from ₹9,000 crore in FY26's revised estimates. Over 43.52 crore Ayushman cards have now been issued.

How the senior top-up actually stacks

The base PM-JAY scheme covers eligible families for ₹5 lakh a year, shared across the whole family. The Vay Vandana Card adds a separate ₹5 lakh specifically for citizens 70 and older — and critically, it isn't means-tested, so it applies even to seniors whose families wouldn't otherwise qualify for base PM-JAY under the income criteria. For a senior in an already-PM-JAY-eligible family, that means ₹10 lakh in total available annual cover; for a senior in a higher-income family, the ₹5 lakh Vay Vandana cover still applies on its own.

Hospital network keeps growing

The empanelled hospital network has passed 32,320 as of early 2026, spanning public and private institutions. Coverage isn't uniform nationally — in states like Madhya Pradesh and Uttar Pradesh, nearly all major private hospitals have joined the panel, giving residents there especially broad access, while coverage density varies elsewhere. It's worth confirming a specific hospital's current empanelment before assuming treatment there will be cashless.

The budget commitment behind the expansion

The Union Budget for 2026-27 raised PM-JAY's allocation to ₹9,500 crore, up from ₹9,000 crore in FY26's revised estimates — a modest but real increase reflecting the scheme's continued growth in enrollment and hospital network size. Over 43.52 crore Ayushman cards have now been created, covering more than 55 crore beneficiaries across the base scheme and the senior top-up combined.

What this means for a household with an elderly parent

  • Apply for the Vay Vandana Card directly if you have a family member 70+ — it isn't automatic and isn't tied to your family's existing PM-JAY status.
  • Don't assume income disqualifies a senior — the Vay Vandana Card specifically removes that barrier, unlike the base scheme.
  • Confirm hospital empanelment before admission where possible, given the network's uneven density across states.

Does every senior citizen qualify for the Vay Vandana Card, or only low-income families?

Every Indian citizen aged 70 and above qualifies for the Vay Vandana Card regardless of income — this is the specific change made in 2024 that expanded PM-JAY beyond its original bottom-40%-of-population eligibility. Younger family members still need to meet the standard PM-JAY income/SECC 2011 eligibility criteria to be covered under the base scheme.

Is the ₹5 lakh senior top-up separate from the family's existing PM-JAY cover?

Yes. The Vay Vandana Card's ₹5 lakh is an addition on top of the family's existing ₹5 lakh PM-JAY cover, taking total available family coverage to ₹10 lakh per year where a senior citizen is part of an already-eligible household. For a senior in a family that wasn't otherwise PM-JAY-eligible, the ₹5 lakh Vay Vandana cover still applies on its own, since it isn't income-restricted.

Where can PM-JAY be used for treatment?

PM-JAY is a cashless scheme usable at over 32,000 empanelled hospitals nationwide, spanning both public and private institutions. Coverage varies somewhat by state — in states like Madhya Pradesh and Uttar Pradesh, nearly all major private hospitals are now part of the panel, but it's worth confirming a specific hospital's empanelment status before treatment rather than assuming universal coverage.
