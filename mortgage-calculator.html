What's included in the monthly payment?

Principal and interest are calculated with the standard fixed-rate amortization formula on the loan amount (home price minus down payment). Property tax and home insurance are simply divided by 12 and added on top, and PMI is estimated as a percentage of the loan balance per year, also divided by 12.

What is PMI and when does it apply?

Private Mortgage Insurance is typically required when the down payment is below 20% of the home price, since the lender is taking on more risk. This calculator applies your entered PMI rate automatically whenever the down payment is under that 20% threshold, and shows zero otherwise.

Does this account for changing interest rates or extra payments?

No — this models a standard fixed-rate mortgage with no extra principal payments. Adjustable-rate mortgages, biweekly payment schedules, or extra prepayments would all change the real total interest paid.
