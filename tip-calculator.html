How is tip calculated?

Multiply the bill by the tip percentage to get the tip amount, then add that to the bill for the total. Divide the total by the number of people for an even split.

What's a normal tip percentage?

15–20% is the typical range in the US for table service; some people go higher for exceptional service or lower for counter service. Adjust the field above to match your situation.

Does this split the tip evenly even if orders were different sizes?

This calculator splits the total bill evenly across everyone entered. For itemized splitting by what each person actually ordered, you'd add up individual amounts first, then use this tool per person.

What if a service charge is already included in the bill?

Check your receipt — if a service charge or gratuity is already listed as a line item, that typically covers the tip, and adding another tip on top is optional (some regions treat it as customary regardless). Enter 0% in this calculator if you just want to confirm the per-person split without adding anything extra.

Should tax be included before or after calculating the tip?

Convention varies — many people tip on the pre-tax subtotal, though tipping on the post-tax total is also common and simpler in practice. Enter whichever amount (pre- or post-tax) matches your own preference in the bill field above.
